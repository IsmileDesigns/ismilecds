Theresa Alexander Inman is an author writing for an audience of educators and parents — people who pick up a book expecting to be guided, not lectured. The cover needed to signal credibility without feeling cold, and warmth without looking self-published.

## The Challenge

Book cover design is deceptively hard. It has to work at thumbnail size on Amazon and full size in a bookstore. It has to communicate genre, tone, and audience in under three seconds. And for a first-time author, it's often the only thing standing between "this looks worth reading" and a scroll-past.

Theresa had a clear sense of her reader — professionals who care deeply about the children in their lives — but needed a designer who could translate that into something that competed with traditionally published titles.

## What We Built

### Cover Concept

We explored directions before landing on a concept that balanced warmth and authority. The final design uses clean, confident typography paired with a visual element that speaks directly to the subject matter without being literal about it.

The colour palette was chosen to feel welcoming on a shelf dominated by either all-white clinical covers or oversaturated stock-photo designs — a deliberate middle ground that makes the book stand out by being considered.

### Typography & Layout

Every type decision was made with legibility at scale in mind. The title reads clearly at thumbnail. The author name is given proper weight — not an afterthought — which matters for building an author brand across future books.

### Print-Ready Delivery

Files were delivered print-ready with full bleed, spine calculations, and back cover layout included — everything the printer needed, nothing for Theresa to figure out on her own.

## The Result

A cover that looks like it belongs in the same category as the books it's sitting next to — which, for a debut author, is exactly the goal. Theresa launched with confidence knowing her first impression was working for her before a single page was read.
